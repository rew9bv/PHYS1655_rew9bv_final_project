# PHYS1655_rew9bv_final_project

The Feeling & Deciding Dataset from Gaia Corazzoli et al. investigates how both objective and subjective factors influence our decision-making process, and to what extent. The data comes from a series of experiments where participants were objectively evaluated on things like reaction times and accuracy, then were asked to evaluate themselves for their subjective feelings like confidence and effort levels. For this project, I decided to investigate the potential relationship between decision/reaction times (objective) and the amount of effort reported (subjective). My question being: Are longer decision times (choice_rt) associated with higher subjective effort (effort_rating)? I found that choice reaction time and effort rating have a weak correlation, meaning longer reaction times are somewhat associated with more perceived (subjective) effort. This may suggest that participants take more time when they perceive a task to require more effort, however, because of the correlation, subjective effort is very likely to be influenced by more than just difficulty in decision-making.
